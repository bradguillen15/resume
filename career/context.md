## What Motivates Me

Technology is not what motivates me.

I enjoy improving systems, simplifying complexity, and reducing maintenance cost. I enjoy understanding products and making software easier to evolve. I gravitate toward products expected to live for years—not throwaway prototypes.

I naturally look for repeated patterns, duplicated code, and architectural improvements. One of my favorite outcomes is deleting code instead of writing more of it.

---

## Engineering Philosophy

I strongly prefer:

- Simplicity over cleverness
- Maintainability over unnecessary abstractions
- Incremental improvements instead of massive rewrites
- Understanding the product before implementing features
- Hiding implementation details behind good abstractions
- Reducing future engineering cost

Technical debt is sometimes acceptable when business benefits outweigh the cost—but it should always be intentional. I do not chase perfect architecture. I optimize for good engineering decisions.

Deeper treatment of principles lives in [`philosophy/engineering.md`](philosophy/engineering.md) and [`philosophy/architecture.md`](philosophy/architecture.md).

## What Makes Me Different

My biggest strength is not a framework—it is engineering judgment.

I naturally think about long-term maintenance, developer experience, future migrations, reusable abstractions, reducing complexity, and making code easier to understand.

When I inherit a codebase, I do not immediately think about adding features. I first try to understand:

- The product
- The architecture
- Recurring patterns
- Unnecessary complexity
- Opportunities for simplification

---

## Working Style

I collaborate closely with designers, product managers, and backend engineers. When implementing a feature, I often suggest UX improvements when I believe they will improve the final product. I do not see my role as simply completing tickets.

I prefer owning areas of the product. When I own something, I feel responsible for its long-term quality—not just shipping the current sprint.

---

## AI Philosophy

I actively use AI as part of my engineering workflow. I do not blindly accept generated code. I review it critically, simplify it, and reject unnecessary complexity.

AI is best used as an accelerator—not a replacement for engineering judgment. I expect software engineers to spend more time reviewing architecture and generated code than writing every line manually.

See also [`philosophy/working-with-ai.md`](philosophy/working-with-ai.md).

## Instructions for AI Assistants

When helping me with anything derived from this repository:

**Accuracy**

- Never exaggerate accomplishments.
- Never invent metrics.
- Prefer concrete examples from [`experience/`](experience/), [`projects/`](projects/), and [`stories/`](stories/) over generic claims.

**Calibration**

**Output quality**

- My engineering identity is defined by ownership, maintainability, simplicity, and judgment—not by a single framework or job title.

**Source of truth**

- Facts belong in leaf documents (`experience/`, `projects/`, `stories/`). Generated views (`resume/`, `interviews/`, `recommendations/`) reference those sources—they do not duplicate them.
