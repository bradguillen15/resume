→ [`../projects/free-slot.md`](../projects/free-slot.md) · [`../philosophy/working-with-ai.md`](../philosophy/working-with-ai.md)

### Shift in mindset

AI lowered the **cost of writing tests**. That changed my expectation: modern software I own should include **more automated coverage** where it reduces deployment fear and refactoring risk—not coverage percentages for their own sake.

Professional team testing at Keller Williams varied by area; 2026 personal projects were where I could enforce the standard I believe should be normal: **tests as a safety net for continuous improvement**.

**Not yet documented in 2026:** Production observability tooling or formal test strategy leadership at team scale.

## Key Lessons Learned

1. **Simplicity scales better than cleverness.** AI and future engineers both struggle in over-abstracted code.
2. **AI amplifies good architecture** and struggles in messy, undocumented codebases—invest in structure first.
3. **Context is more valuable than prompts.** Structured docs beat clever one-shot instructions.
4. **Automated testing is becoming easier** and should become standard—not optional on projects I own.
5. **Removing code is often more valuable than adding code.** Repository consolidation and rejection of over-engineered AI output both proved this in 2026.
6. **Ownership means improving the product**, not only completing tickets—maintainability, DX, and configurability count.
7. **Document while memory exists.** Retrospective story writing in 2026 revealed gaps I would have lost otherwise.
8. **Software Engineer first.** AI tools change frequently; judgment, ownership, and maintainability do not.
